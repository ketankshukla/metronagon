---

A brilliantly clever book cover that depicts an isometric top-down view of a chessboard — but instead of chess pieces, the board is populated with miniature crime scene elements rendered in the clean, precise style of isometric architectural illustration. The chessboard becomes a crime scene diagram: the squares become rooms, the pieces become people, and the positions tell a story of murder, investigation, and strategy. The rendering is crisp, geometric, and precisely projected — every element drawn in true isometric perspective where parallel lines remain parallel, creating the characteristic "3D diagram" look of strategy games and architectural plans.

THE CHESSBOARD (filling 15-85% from top):

An 8x8 chessboard viewed from a slightly elevated isometric angle — looking down at approximately 30 degrees from directly above. The board is rendered in isometric projection: the squares appear as parallelograms, creating a three-dimensional appearance while maintaining the geometric precision of a technical diagram.

LIGHT SQUARES: Pale marble cream (#F0E8D0) with subtle stone veining
DARK SQUARES: Deep charcoal grey (#2A2A2A) with a matte finish

The board rests on a dark surface that extends to the edges of the image — deep navy (#0A0A1A), clean and flat.

THE "PIECES" — MINIATURE CRIME SCENE ELEMENTS:

Instead of traditional chess pieces, the board is populated with tiny, isometric-rendered elements that tell a murder mystery:

THE KING (white, positioned on e1): A tiny miniature DETECTIVE figure — a man in a trench coat and fedora, rendered in clean isometric style at approximately 3% of image height. He stands on the square facing the center of the board, a magnifying glass in one hand. His colors are warm: tan trench coat (#C8A878), dark hat.

THE QUEEN (black, positioned on d8): A tiny miniature SUSPECT figure — an elegant woman in a long dark dress, rendered in the same isometric style. She stands poised, one hand raised, her posture ambiguous between innocence and guilt. Deep crimson dress (#6A1A1A).

THE VICTIM (positioned on d5, the center of the board): Not a standing figure but a CHALK OUTLINE — a small white outline of a human body drawn on the dark square, exactly as at a crime scene. This is the board's emotional center — the body around which all other pieces are positioned. Next to the chalk outline, a tiny numbered evidence marker (#FFD040 yellow triangle with the number "1").

ROOKS (as buildings): Two miniature isometric BUILDINGS on the corner squares — tiny architectural models: one resembles a manor house (the crime scene location), the other resembles a police station. Clean geometric forms with tiny windows and doors.

BISHOPS (as weapons): Tiny isometric objects on diagonal squares — a miniature revolver on one diagonal, a miniature candlestick on another. Classic whodunit murder weapons, rendered as precise little isometric objects casting tiny shadows.

KNIGHTS (as vehicles): A miniature isometric police car (black and white with a tiny light bar) and a miniature dark sedan (the suspect's car) on their respective squares. Clean, geometric, toy-like.

PAWNS (as evidence): Scattered across the front ranks, tiny isometric evidence items: a miniature envelope, a small key, a tiny photograph (just a white rectangle at this scale), a medicine bottle, a torn piece of paper, a pocket watch, a pair of glasses, and a small book. Eight items, one per pawn square, each one a clue in the mystery.

ADDITIONAL CRIME SCENE DETAILS:

- CRIME SCENE TAPE: Yellow (#FFD040) tape stretched between tiny stanchions on two adjacent squares, blocking off the victim's area
- RED ARROW LINES: Thin red (#CC2020) directional arrows drawn on the board surface (like arrows on a crime scene diagram) showing the movements of suspects — dotted lines from the Queen's position toward the victim, solid lines from the Detective's position toward various evidence items
- NUMBERED MARKERS: Small yellow evidence markers next to each pawn-evidence item, numbered 1-8
- GRID REFERENCES: Along the board edges, chess notation labels (a-h horizontally, 1-8 vertically) rendered in small, clean sans-serif — serving as both chess coordinates and crime scene grid references

ISOMETRIC RENDERING STYLE:

- All elements are drawn in TRUE ISOMETRIC PROJECTION — no perspective convergence, all vertical lines remain vertical, all horizontal parallels remain parallel at 30-degree angles
- Clean, crisp lines with consistent weight
- Flat color fills with minimal shading — shadows rendered as a single flat darker tone beneath each object, projected to the lower-right
- No texture — all surfaces are clean flat color, as in a technical diagram
- Tiny but precise detail — each miniature element is small but clearly identifiable
