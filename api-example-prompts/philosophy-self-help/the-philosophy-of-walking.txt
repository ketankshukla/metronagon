---

A elegant, minimalist book cover where the entire illustration is drawn with a SINGLE CONTINUOUS LINE — one unbroken stroke that begins at the bottom of the cover and ends at the top, drawing a walking figure, a landscape, and a philosophical journey without the pen ever leaving the paper. The continuous-line constraint creates a visual metaphor for walking itself: one step follows another, the path never breaks, and the journey from start to finish is one uninterrupted flow. The line is thin, confident, and beautiful — the kind of drawing that looks effortless but requires extraordinary skill.

THE CONTINUOUS LINE (the entire illustration):

A single black line (#1A1A1A) begins at the bottom-center of the image (approximately 90% from top) and travels upward through the entire cover, drawing everything it encounters without ever breaking contact with the paper:

THE WALKING FIGURE (lower portion, 65-85% from top):
The line begins by drawing a person mid-stride — one foot forward, one foot back, arms slightly swinging, body leaning into the walk. The figure is drawn in the continuous-line style: the pen traces the outline of one shoe, travels up the leg, across the hip, down the other leg to the other shoe, then back up through the torso, out along one arm, back to the shoulder, up the neck, around the head (a simple oval profile with a suggestion of features — a nose, an eye, drawn as the line passes), and continues upward from the top of the head.

The figure is SIMPLE but recognizable — the continuous-line constraint means the drawing cannot be highly detailed, but the gesture and proportions are confident and expressive. The figure is clearly walking, clearly alive, clearly going somewhere.

THE PATH (from the figure upward, 40-65% from top):
From the top of the walking figure, the line continues upward and begins to draw the path ahead — a winding trail that curves left, then right, then left again as it ascends the page. The path is drawn as two roughly parallel lines (the line traces one edge of the path going up, then curves back to trace the other edge going down, before continuing upward again). Along the path, the line draws small details without breaking:
- Pebbles: small loops and circles along the path edges
- Grass: short zigzag strokes beside the path
- A small puddle: an oval loop reflecting nothing

THE LANDSCAPE (middle portion, 25-55% from top):
As the line continues upward drawing the path, it also draws the surrounding landscape — the line detours to sketch elements before returning to the path:
- TREES: The line loops outward to draw the trunk (a vertical stroke up, a curve at the top) and the canopy (a series of connected loops creating a cloud-like tree crown) of several trees along the path. Each tree is drawn with the minimum strokes the continuous-line constraint allows — some are mere suggestions (a vertical line with a scribbled top), others are more developed.
- HILLS: The line traces the rolling contour of distant hills — sweeping, gentle curves that rise and fall across the background. These hill lines are among the most beautiful in the drawing — long, smooth, flowing curves.
- A STREAM: The line crosses over a small stream by drawing a simple arched bridge (or stepping stones) — a few quick loops that suggest water and crossing.

THE SKY AND DESTINATION (upper portion, 15-35% from top):
The line continues upward into the sky, drawing:
- CLOUDS: Simple, flowing loops that create cumulus cloud shapes — each cloud is a series of connected curves, drawn without lifting the pen
- BIRDS: Two or three birds in the distance — the classic M-shaped shorthand for birds in flight, each one a small, quick dip in the continuous line
- The line's final journey: upward toward the top of the page, thinning slightly as though the pen is being gradually lifted (the line weight decreases from solid to lighter), until it terminates in a single small POINT or SPIRAL at approximately 12% from top — the journey's end, or perhaps its beginning.

LINE QUALITY:

The single line has varying qualities along its length, all achieved without lifting the pen:
- CONFIDENT STRAIGHT SECTIONS: Where the path is clear, the line is smooth and relatively straight
- FLOWING CURVES: The hills, clouds, and tree canopies are drawn with long, elegant curves
- QUICK LOOPS: Small details (pebbles, leaves, water ripples) are drawn as rapid, small loops
- VARYING WEIGHT: The line is slightly thicker where the pen pressed harder (the figure's grounding foot, the base of tree trunks) and thinner where it pressed lightly (distant hills, sky elements). This weight variation is subtle but gives the drawing life.
- SPEED VARIATION: Some sections are drawn slowly (the figure's face, the path's curves) and some quickly (the grass, the bird shapes) — the speed is visible in the line's character.

THE PAPER:

Warm cream (#FFF8EE) — the clean, warm surface of quality drawing paper. The paper has a very subtle tooth (texture) that the pen line responds to — the line is not perfectly smooth but has the micro-variation of ink on textured paper. The paper is otherwise blank — no grid, no marks, no stains. The cleanliness of the paper emphasizes the single line's journey across it.

- Paper cream: #FFF8EE — the background
That's it. Two tones. The constraint is absolute.

"of WALKING" — on the next line, same font, slightly LARGER, in the same warm dark grey. The title is quiet, literary, philosophical.

OVERALL MOOD:

Contemplative, flowing, liberating — the feeling of setting out on a long walk with no destination, knowing that the walk itself is the purpose. The continuous-line constraint mirrors the unbroken flow of walking — one step follows another, the path curves and rises and descends, but it never breaks. The minimalism (one line, two colors) creates a cover that is both simple and profound — there is nothing to distract from the line's journey, just as walking strips away distraction and leaves only the rhythm of steps.

THIS IS SINGLE CONTINUOUS LINE DRAWING. One unbroken line from bottom to top, drawing everything it encounters. The beauty is in the constraint — what can you draw without ever lifting the pen? A figure, a path, a landscape, a journey, a philosophy.
