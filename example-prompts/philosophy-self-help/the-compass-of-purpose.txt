A warm, beautifully detailed book cover depicting a single antique brass compass — rendered as a precision technical illustration with the obsessive detail of a Victorian instrument maker's catalog. The compass is shown face-up, filling most of the cover, its brass case, glass crystal, and engraved compass rose rendered with metallic precision. The key visual twist: the compass needle does not point to "N" but to a custom cardinal direction engraved as "PURPOSE" — transforming a navigation instrument into a life metaphor.

The compass face is the cover's visual centerpiece — an elaborate, engraved compass rose in the traditional style:

THE ROSE DESIGN: A classic 32-point compass rose — the elaborate star pattern with primary, secondary, and tertiary directional points. The rose is engraved into the brass base of the compass and filled with enamel:
- The four CARDINAL POINTS (N, S, E, W) are the largest — broad, pointed diamond shapes extending to near the rim
- The four INTERCARDINAL POINTS (NE, NW, SE, SW) are medium-sized
- The eight SECONDARY INTERCARDINALS and sixteen TERTIARY points are progressively smaller
- The traditional coloring: the north point is BLUE enamel (#1A3A6A), the east point is RED enamel (#8B1A1A), and the remaining points alternate between gold (#D4AF37) and cream (#FFF0D0)

THE CUSTOM DIRECTIONS: Instead of the standard cardinal letters, the four primary directions are engraved with custom text:
- NORTH position: "PURPOSE" — engraved in serif capitals, the direction the needle points
- SOUTH position: "FEAR" — the direction away from purpose
- EAST position: "GROWTH" — the direction of rising
- WEST position: "COMFORT" — the direction of setting (staying safe)

These engraved words are rendered in the fine, precise lettering of brass engraving — thin, serif characters cut into the metal surface, filled with dark enamel for legibility.

THE NEEDLE: A blued-steel compass needle (#2A3A5A with bright blue-steel highlights) — the characteristic dark blue of heat-treated steel. The needle is long and thin, with a pointed north-seeking end (tipped with a small luminous dot in pale green — the radium-paint dot of vintage compasses) and a broader counterbalance end. The needle points precisely toward "PURPOSE" at the north position.

THE PIVOT: At the center, a small jeweled pivot — a tiny, bright point (ruby red #CC2020) set in a brass cap. The jewel bearing is the compass's mechanical heart — a real ruby or synthetic gem that reduces friction.

DEGREE MARKINGS: Around the inner edge of the compass rim, tiny degree markings (0-360) are engraved — precise, evenly spaced, with longer marks every 10 degrees and numbers every 30 degrees. These markings are extremely fine and add to the instrument's precision aesthetic.
